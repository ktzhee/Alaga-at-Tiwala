Protection Services

Protection Services connect people with organizations that prevent abuse, respond to crises, and promote human rights and safety.

They may include legal protection, shelter, psychosocial support, emergency response, and referral to specialized services in health, housing, and justice systems.

Government Agencies

Government agencies are official public institutions mandated to protect citizens’ rights, uphold the law, and coordinate emergency response. They typically manage police and rescue services, social welfare programs, child and family protection, and mechanisms for reporting abuse, exploitation, or corruption.

Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD)

The DSWD is the lead national agency for social protection, disaster response, and services for vulnerable groups such as children, persons with disabilities, and poor families. It runs crisis intervention units, community-based programs, and supervises attached agencies that deliver residential care, adoption, and child and youth welfare services.

Philippine National Police – Women and Children Protection Center (PNP WCPC)

The PNP WCPC is the specialized police unit that investigates crimes involving violence, abuse, and exploitation of women and children. It works closely with social workers and prosecutors to document cases, rescue victims, and build criminal cases against perpetrators.

Commission on Human Rights of the Philippines (CHR)

The CHR is the independent constitutional office mandated to investigate human rights violations, monitor state compliance with human rights obligations, and assist victims seeking accountability. It receives complaints, conducts fact‑finding missions, and coordinates with law‑enforcement, courts, and social welfare offices to protect civil, political, and socio‑economic rights.

Philippine Commission on Women (PCW)

The Philippine Commission on Women is the national policy‑making and coordinating body on women and gender equality, tasked with integrating gender concerns into all government plans and programs. It promotes the economic, social, and political empowerment of women, monitors implementation of laws and international commitments on women’s rights, and provides technical assistance to agencies on gender‑responsive and protective services for women and girls.
