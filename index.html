Background

A lack of access to proper family planning remains a critical issue in the Philippines and worldwide. Many families face challenges such as unexpected pregnancies, which can lead to harmful effects such as poor financial stability, mental health issues, and increased risks during childbirth. These factors contribute to the development of unstable families and stressful home environments, which may sometimes escalate to domestic violence.

Efforts have been made to address these issues. These include the:

Workplace family planning program at Hamlin Industrial Corporation in Cavite

In the workplace, 88% of female workers utilized modern contraceptive methods, which led to better family planning and economic stability for employees. The program includes voluntary counseling and free access to contraceptives, empowering women to control the timing and size of their families, which also benefits the company's productivity.

Department of Health outreach in Mandaluyong City

300 residents were given free family planning services, including consultations, contraceptive distribution, and implant procedures.

These efforts show that progress is being made; however, gaps in resources, education, and legal support persist. And until now, numerous children and families are still affected nationwide.
